About

I am a Ph.D. student in Water Science & Management at New Mexico State University, with a concentration in Water Economics & Policy. My academic background also includes doctoral training in economic development. My work lies in applied microeconomics, environmental economics, natural resource economics, and policy analysis.

My research focuses on how institutions, regulation, and governance shape water, energy, and environmental outcomes. I am particularly interested in policy-relevant empirical work that connects economic analysis with real-world questions in resource management and public policy.

Teaching

I approach teaching with the view that students learn best when courses are clear, structured, and welcoming. Across undergraduate economics courses, I aim to help students build confidence in economic reasoning while feeling comfortable asking questions and engaging with the material.

My teaching experience includes service as both instructor of record and teaching assistant at New Mexico State University and the University of New Mexico.
